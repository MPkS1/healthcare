Symptoms

Nausea

Symptoms Overview

  • Sensation of discomfort or unease in the stomach
  • May be accompanied by an urge to vomit or actual vomiting
  • Often triggered by various factors including infections, medications, motion sickness, or pregnancy

Potential Overview

Nausea can be a symptom of various underlying conditions, including:

  • Viral infections like influenza, norovirus, or hepatitis
  • Bacterial infections such as food poisoning or gastroenteritis
  • Medication side effects
  • Motion sickness or seasickness
  • Migraines or tension headaches
  • Pregnancy-related morning sickness

Immediate Remedies

  • Stay hydrated by drinking small amounts of clear fluids
  • Eat bland foods like crackers or toast
  • Avoid spicy, greasy, or heavy foods
  • Take over-the-counter medications like antacids or anti-nausea drugs
  • Rest in a comfortable position

When to Seek Medical Help

While nausea is often temporary and self-limiting, medical attention may be necessary in certain situations:

  • If nausea persists for more than 24 hours
  • If nausea is accompanied by severe abdominal pain or fever
  • If nausea is recurrent and interferes with daily activities
  • If nausea is associated with dehydration or weight loss
  • If nausea occurs during pregnancy and is severe or persistent
  • If nausea is accompanied by other concerning symptoms like chest pain, dizziness, or fainting

Seeking medical help can help identify the underlying cause of nausea and determine appropriate treatment.

Self Care Tips

  • Rest in a quiet and comfortable environment
  • Eat small, frequent meals
  • Avoid strong odors or triggers that may worsen nausea
  • Use relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation
  • Stay hydrated by sipping clear fluids throughout the day
  • Avoid lying down immediately after eating
  • Avoid consuming large meals or spicy, fatty, or fried foods
  • Consider ginger supplements or ginger tea to alleviate nausea

Nausea

Nausea is a sensation of discomfort or unease in the stomach that often precedes vomiting. It can be triggered by various factors including infections, medications, motion sickness, or pregnancy. Nausea may be temporary and resolve on its own, but persistent or severe nausea may indicate an underlying medical condition that requires attention.
