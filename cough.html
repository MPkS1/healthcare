Symptoms

Cough

Symptoms Overview

  • Expelling air from the lungs with a sudden sharp sound
  • May be accompanied by mucus production, chest discomfort, or throat irritation
  • Can occur due to various factors including viral or bacterial infections, allergies, or irritants

Potential Overview

Cough can be a symptom of various underlying conditions, including:

  • Viral respiratory infections like the common cold or flu
  • Bacterial infections such as bronchitis or pneumonia
  • Allergic reactions to pollen, dust, or pet dander
  • Asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Exposure to irritants like cigarette smoke or air pollution
  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)

Immediate Remedies

  • Stay hydrated by drinking warm fluids like herbal tea or broth
  • Use cough drops or lozenges to soothe throat irritation
  • Avoid irritants like smoke, strong odors, or pollutants
  • Try over-the-counter cough suppressants or expectorants
  • Use a humidifier to add moisture to the air and ease coughing

When to Seek Medical Help

While coughing is often a temporary and self-limiting symptom, medical attention may be necessary in certain situations:

  • If cough persists for more than three weeks
  • If cough is accompanied by fever, chest pain, or difficulty breathing
  • If coughing up blood or thick yellow or green mucus
  • If cough is severe and disrupts sleep or daily activities
  • If cough occurs in individuals with pre-existing lung conditions like asthma or COPD
  • If cough is associated with unintentional weight loss or fatigue

Medical evaluation can help determine the underlying cause of the cough and guide appropriate treatment.

Self Care Tips

  • Get plenty of rest to support the immune system
  • Avoid exposure to cigarette smoke or other respiratory irritants
  • Use a cough syrup or honey to soothe throat irritation
  • Stay hydrated by drinking water or warm beverages
  • Use a vaporizer or take a steamy shower to ease congestion
  • Elevate your head while sleeping to reduce nighttime coughing
  • Avoid dairy products, as they can increase mucus production in some individuals
  • If cough persists, consult with a healthcare professional for further evaluation and management

Cough

A cough is a reflex action that helps clear your airways of irritants and mucus. It can be caused by various factors, including infections, allergies, or irritants. While coughing is usually temporary and harmless, persistent or severe coughing may indicate an underlying medical condition that requires attention.
