Symptoms

Fever

Symptoms Overview

  • Elevated body temperature above the normal range
  • May be accompanied by chills, sweating, headache, muscle aches, and fatigue
  • Body temperature elevation is a natural response to infections, inflammation, or other medical conditions

Potential Overview

Fever can be a symptom of various underlying conditions, including:

  • Viral infections like influenza, common cold, or COVID-19
  • Bacterial infections such as urinary tract infection, strep throat, or pneumonia
  • Inflammatory conditions like rheumatoid arthritis or inflammatory bowel disease
  • Heat exhaustion or heatstroke
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Reaction to certain medications
  • Cancer

Immediate Remedies

  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids
  • Rest in a cool environment
  • Apply cool compresses or take a lukewarm bath to reduce body temperature
  • Over-the-counter fever reducers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen
  • Avoid excessive physical activity or exertion

When to Seek Medical Help

While fever is often a normal immune response and resolves on its own, medical attention may be necessary under certain circumstances:

  • If fever persists for more than three days
  • If fever is accompanied by severe headache, stiff neck, confusion, or difficulty breathing
  • If fever occurs in infants under three months of age
  • If fever is recurrent or occurs in individuals with compromised immune systems
  • If fever is associated with rash, vomiting, or persistent abdominal pain
  • If fever interferes with daily activities or worsens despite home remedies

Medical evaluation can help determine the underlying cause of fever and guide appropriate treatment.

Self Care Tips

  • Get plenty of rest and sleep to support the immune system
  • Drink fluids like water, herbal tea, or electrolyte solutions to stay hydrated
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins
  • Avoid alcohol, caffeine, and sugary beverages
  • Monitor body temperature regularly
  • Practice good hygiene to prevent the spread of infections
  • Wear lightweight clothing and use lightweight bedding to stay comfortable
  • Consider using a humidifier to ease breathing and soothe respiratory symptoms

Fever

Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, often in response to an infection or illness. It is a common symptom of various conditions and is typically accompanied by other symptoms like chills, sweating, headache, and muscle aches.
